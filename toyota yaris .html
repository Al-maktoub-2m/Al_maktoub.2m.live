The Toyota Yaris is a popular compact car manufactured by the Japanese automaker Toyota since 1999. It is available in many versions, each with its own features and options. Here are some additional details about the different versions of the Toyota Yaris:

L: The Yaris L is the base version of the Toyota Yaris. It is equipped with a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 106 horsepower. The car comes with a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission. Standard features of the Yaris L include cloth seats, a 7-inch touchscreen, a six-speaker audio system, and automatic headlights.

Yaris LE: The Yaris LE is a higher-end version of the car. It comes with the same basic features as the Yaris L, but with a few additions. The Yaris LE has an infotainment system with a 7-inch touchscreen and compatibility with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. It also has alloy wheels, LED daytime running lights, and LED taillights.

Yaris XLE: The Yaris XLE is the top-of-the-line version of the Toyota Yaris. It is equipped with a 1.5-liter engine that produces 106 horsepower and is paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. Standard features of the Yaris XLE include leather seats, an eight-speaker audio system, a navigation system, 16-inch alloy wheels, LED daytime running lights, and LED taillights.

Yaris SE Nightshade Edition: The Yaris SE Nightshade Edition is a special version of the car that features black aesthetic features. It is equipped with black 16-inch alloy wheels, black fog lights, a black rear spoiler, and other black accents. The Yaris SE Nightshade Edition also comes with a six-speaker audio system, a 7-inch touchscreen, and sport cloth seats.

Yaris GR Sport: The Yaris GR Sport is a sporty version of the Toyota Yaris. It is equipped with a 1.5-liter gasoline engine paired with an electric motor that produces a combined output of 116 horsepower. The Yaris GR Sport comes with 17-inch alloy wheels, sport suspension, sport seats, and an eight-speaker audio system.

Yaris Cross: The Yaris Cross is a compact crossover based on the Yaris platform. It is equipped with a 1.5-liter gasoline engine that produces 120 horsepower. The Yaris Cross is available in both two-wheel and four-wheel drive versions and comes with a 7-inch infotainment system with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto compatibility.

More dynamic than truly sporty, the second-generation Yaris TS reconciles beautiful performance with great compactness, giving it a rare versatility while remaining pleasant and reliable! Toyota succeeded with its first Yaris, launched in 1998. Nicely designed, solidly built in France, it was very successful. Its descendant, revealed at the 2005 Frankfurt Motor Show and still assembled in Onnaing, capitalizes on the qualities that so pleased customers: very compact bodywork to facilitate parking, clever interior with a sliding bench, nice look, and serious manufacturing. Besides, the category is not the same, with the Toyota being priced much lower when it was released in February 2007: €16,580 for the 3-door version, which is equivalent to €20,500 today according to INSEE. For an additional €1,500, one can purchase the 5-door version. The equipment is complete: automatic air conditioning, MP3 radio, 17-inch alloy wheels, keyless start, ESP, electric windows and mirrors. As an option, GPS, rear parking sensor, or Bluetooth are available. However, the Suzuki Swift Sport, which already offers 125 hp, is €1,000 cheaper. Despite this, Toyota did not have high commercial ambitions for the Yaris TS, hoping to sell only a small thousand units annually in France. It disappeared at the end of 2010 when the Yaris was renewed. However, it wasn't until late 2006 that the TS sport version was introduced. It swaps the 105 hp 1.5 L engine of its predecessor for a 133 hp 1.8 L engine. This naturally aspirated engine uses two continuously variable camshaft phasers (double VVTi system) to develop its horsepower, at a reasonable engine speed of 6,000 rpm. The maximum torque of 173 Nm is reached at 4,400 rpm, which is rather high, but the Franco-Japanese car weighs only 1,120 kg. As a result, the 0 to 100 km/h time of 9.3 seconds seems quite interesting, even though the Yaris TS will not challenge a Renault Clio III RS, naturally.

What to watch out for?

Like many Toyotas, the Yaris TS is known for its great reliability, provided it has been properly maintained. Its engine, equipped with a chain drive, does not suffer from any notable flaws, nor does its gearbox. However, recalls have taken place concerning the steering column, sound insulation, or accelerator pedal. There have also been a few cases of oxidized ABS sensors, but nothing serious. Overall, few city cars, especially sporty ones, provide such a serene driving experience as this Yaris! Behind the wheel Onboard the Toyota Yaris 1.8 TS, one is not particularly impressed by the appearance of the plastics. Still, the functionality of the dashboard, containing three closed storage compartments and water bottle holders, is impressive! In the back, one appreciates the sliding bench seat and the double-floor trunk, even though space is just average. In the front, one enjoys an excellent driving position (adjustable steering wheel in both planes) and a comfortable seat. How much does it cost? The Yaris TS does not lose much value and can be found in good condition for as low as €5,800, with more than 150,000 km on the clock. At €6,500, the mileage drops to around 100,000 km, while at €7,500, one can get a car with approximately 80,000 km.

The engine is lively and also stands out for its flexibility in the city, combined with a certain smoothness, like the gearbox. On the road, it ensures good acceleration, even in 5th gear, but you have to wait until you reach 4,000 rpm to get some character. Nothing like the block of a Swift Sport, much more aggressive. And since the final ratio is short (31.8 km/h per 1,000 rpm), the Yaris is quite noisy on the highway, even though it remains very tolerable. The chassis is on a par with the engine: efficient but not very sporty. The Toyota will gently place its tail if you force it during cornering entry and will show some agility, but above all it is cautious. Toyota Yaris 1.5 TS (2001 - 2006)

As the first slightly sporty Yaris, the TS emphasizes vitality rather than pure performance, with its 106 hp. And it works: it is very fun to drive, more so than its descendant. On the other hand, it falls far short in terms of comfort, soundproofing, and equipment. In other words, it will be more rewarding than the second-generation TS in sporty driving but more tiring in daily use. Fortunately, it is very reliable, although it will be slightly more vulnerable to corrosion due to its age. Slightly restyled in 2003, it can be purchased starting from €3,000.

The Toyota Yaris TS is a sporty subcompact car that offers impressive performance, handling, and fuel efficiency. It is a well-rounded vehicle that combines practicality and fun-to-drive characteristics, making it an excellent choice for drivers who want a car that can serve as a daily driver but also deliver an engaging driving experience. In terms of design, the Yaris TS has a sleek and stylish exterior that is both modern and sporty. The interior is well-designed, with high-quality materials and comfortable seating. The car also comes with a range of features and technologies that enhance the driving experience, including a touchscreen infotainment system, a rearview camera, and advanced safety features. Under the hood, the Yaris TS is powered by a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine that delivers 106 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a six-speed manual transmission that provides smooth and precise shifts. The car also comes with a sport-tuned suspension and larger brakes, which contribute to its agile handling and responsive driving dynamics.

Overall, the Toyota Yaris TS is a well-engineered and well-executed subcompact car that offers a great balance of performance, efficiency, and practicality. It is a worthy choice for drivers who want a car that is not only reliable and efficient but also fun to drive.
